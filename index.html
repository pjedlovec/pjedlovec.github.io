Home Page for Phillip (PJ) Jedlovec

My main research area is algebraic topology, specifically unstable homotopy theory. I study the truncated Brown-Peterson spectra, their generalizations, and how to use Hopf rings to compute the generalized homology of the spaces in their Ω-spectra.  I am also interested in philosophy of mathematics and areas of intersection between mathematics and economics, such as game theory and social choice theory, and I am deeply passionate about teaching in innovative and effective ways.
